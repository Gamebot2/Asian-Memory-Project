Dorothy Lê

Flutist and Project Lead

Dorothy Lê is a BA music student at the University of Texas at Austin where she studies flute with Professor Marianne Gedigian and is completing her capstone project with Professor James Gabrillo. She is the founder and director of AMP, an idea that started from creating a simple recital where her parents can connect with the music. You'll see her play the flute at events in flat shoes because she refuses to wear heels. In her free time, you can catch her eating spicy instant noodles or rewatching her favorite show, Avatar: The Last Airbender. As a flutist, she should probably be an airbender, but deep down, Dorothy is actually a waterbender.

Jingchao Wang

Composer

Jingchao Wang (b. 1994) is a US-based composer who has been living in China, Germany and the US. Her music is influenced by Eclecticism with numerous Eastern elements. In 2009, Jingchao was accepted to the composition department of the Central Conservatory of Music High School with the highest overall ranking. Three years later, she was admitted by the Central Conservatory of Music as the top-ranked student where she studied with Prof. Xiaogang Ye. During her undergraduate studies, Jingchao was exchanged in Hochschule für Musik und Theater Hamburg, Germany, as the first composition bachelor student in CCOM with the support of the National Scholarship honored by the China Ministry of Education. During her time in Hamburg, she hosted the concert “Imagine and Reflection” to popularize Chinese contemporary music. Jingchao pursued her Master’s degree in Composition at the Peabody Institute of Johns Hopkins University, where she studied in the studio of Professor Michael Hersch. Jingchao is currently an Assistant Instructor at The University of Texas at Austin, meanwhile doing her DMA degree in Composition.

Selina Xu

Cellist

Second prize winner of the Florida Keys International Music Competition, Yilin (Selina) Xu had her solo debut at the Carnegie’s Weill Recital Hall at the age of 17. As an international student, Xu appeared with the Shanghai Symphony Orchestra, and was a member of the Florida Symphony Youth Orchestra (FSYO), Florida Orchestra Association (FOA) of All-State, and National Orchestra of China (NYO China) of 2020. Xu has attended several music festival around Europe, such as the Internationaux d’Orchestra de Jeunes, the Summa Cum Laude International Youth Music Festival. Miss. Xu participated in various cello and chamber masterclasses with Si-Yan Li, David Bjella, Richard Aaron, Jason Wang, Jamie Clark, Alan Rafferty, Hai-ye Ni, the Callisto String Quartet, and Miró Quartet. She is currently studying at the University of Texas at Austin with cellist Bion Tsang.

Ellen Okamura

Graphic Designer

Ellen Okamura is a Design BFA and Business Minor at the University of Texas at Austin, who specializes in branding design and UI design. She is finishing up her senior year with her capstone design project focused on Asian-American female empowerment. Ellen is currently the lead designer for AMP and contributes to the branding identity, as well as the website and marketing graphics. As a Japanese American, she was raised on Studio Ghibli movies, and owns almost all of them on DVD. Her favorite Ghibli movie to this day remains “Spirited Away”. A fun fact about Ellen is that she is actually pretty small in stature, however, she makes up for it with a big personality and her trademark heels.

Moegi Amano

Pianist

Moegi Amano is pursuing her Master of Music at the University of Texas at Austin, where she also completed her bachelor in 2020. She studies under Anton Nel and is the pianist for AMP. Although originally from Japan, she grew up in multiple places, including her hometown Yokkaichi, Kyoto, Kentucky, and Singapore, and considers herself a third culture kid. Through AMP, she enjoys being able to connect with others from Asian backgrounds and feels fortunate to share her love for Ghibli and for music with the audience.
